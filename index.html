My goal is to contribute to credible data systems that generate meaningful insights, support business decisions, and create real-life positive impact.

Secondary Market Condo Listings
Context

Secondary‑market real estate data is highly fragmented, with inconsistent listing standards and limited historical depth. This project examines the feasibility of price‑based analysis using publicly available listings under realistic market constraints.

Objective

To construct a structured dataset of available secondary‑market condominium units in Makati within a 1–3M PHP budget, and assess whether listing‑level data alone is sufficient to support value‑oriented decision analysis.

Scope
  • ~30 condominium developments in Makati
  • Currently listed units for sale only
  • Manually collected from public sources
  • No proprietary or transaction‑level data
Methodology
  • Manual data collection and validation
  • Standardization of price, unit attributes, and development identifiers
  • Identification of missing, non‑comparable, and inconsistent fields
Key Observations
  • Listing completeness varies significantly across developments
  • Asking prices without transaction history limit fair‑value inference
  • Additional temporal and contextual data is required for valuation
Analytical Boundaries

This project does not perform price prediction, valuation modeling, or investment recommendation. Its focus is on data readiness, comparability, and analytical feasibility.

Next Steps
  • Incorporate historical transaction or price‑change data
  • Track listing duration and revisions over time
  • Augment with location‑level and development‑level context
